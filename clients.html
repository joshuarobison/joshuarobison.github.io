When I first discovered Pilates as a form of exercise for me; I didn’t realize how versatile Pilates is a way to become fit. I had read that Pilates could strengthen my core and increase my balance; which my body desperately needed. I didn’t know it has other benefits, as well. It helps to improve one’s posture; it can decrease back pain (which I can certainly attest to that fact.) It helps to prevent further injuries (of which I have brought on a few injuries to my aging body.) It increases one’s energy and enhances body awareness, making a body, mind, spirit connection. I actually look forward to doing Pilates every week. I can’t say that about other forms of exercise.

The first time I met Brooke Claveria 7 years ago, and she introduced me to Pilates; I knew I was entering a new adventure; making exercise fun and enjoyable. At first, when I initially saw the Reformer table, I thought it resembles a torture rack. But I have come to appreciate all of the different strengthening exercises and stretching that can be done, above and beyond what I can do on a mat and roller on the ground. I take private lessons from Brooke at her home studio once/week; and attend a Pilates class at Shoreline Park another day during the week. We use mats, rollers, bands and blocks to augment the exercise repertoire.

I heartedly recommend Brooke’s Pilates classes and private lessons. It is so worth what you put into it, as far as time and money. Try it and see for yourself.
